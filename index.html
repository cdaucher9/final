THE THREAD OF CLASS STRUGGLE

Throughout global history, conflict has often resulted from class struggle.
Wars, revolutions, and protests have defined, created, and destroyed entire civilizations.
We often hear the phrase "history repeats itself" and maybe even ask ourselves why.
But what is the thread that links these cycles together? Could social classes be what
divides us? The below timeline is meant to illustrate historical proletarian revolutions.

The American Revolution
(The Revolutionary War)
(1765–1783)

Join, or Die by Benjamin Franklin (1754)

The Revolutionary War was fought by the British colonies for independence from Great Britain. The cartoon above was created to encourage unity among the colonies as they attempted to gain support to break away from the oppressive British monarchy.

The French Revolution
(1789–1799)

The Death of Jean Paul Marat by Jacques-Louis David (1793)

Jean Paul Marat was a "martyr of the revolution" and was assassinated by a woman named Charlotte Corday.

The Haitian Revolution
(1791–1804)

Burning of the Plaine du Cap - Massacre of whites by the blacks Depicted by the French Military (1833)

The Haitian Revolution was a slave rebellion against the French in what is now Haiti. The revolution resulted in the abolition of slavery and Haiti becoming an independent territory.

The Peninsular War
(1808-1814)

The Third of May Francisco Goya (1808)

This painting was made in honor of those who lost their lives fighting in the war. The war was part of the Napoleonic Wars.

July Revolution
(The Second French Revolution)
(1791–1804)

Liberty Leading the People by Eugène Delacroix (1830)

The July Revolution was a revolution of the people who overthrew King Charles X. The July Revolution marks a shift in power.

The 1848 Revolution
(The February Revolution)
(February–December 1848)

The Uprising by Honoré Daumier (1848)

Theorized to be the origin of the raised (or clenched) fist, this image shows the rise of the working class in France against King Louis Philippe.

Click here to learn more about the 1848 Revolution.

German Revolutions of 1848–1849
(March Revolutions)
(1848–1849)

Unknown Creator (circa 1848–1850)

The March Revolutions were one of many revolutions and rebellions that occurred across Europe during 1848. This revolution was sparked by the conflicting desires of the middle-class and the working-class in Germany. The working-class desired radical improvements of both working and living conditions.

Click here to read more about the various German Revolutions of 1848.

(1910-1920)

The Arsenal by Diego Rivera (1928)

The Mexican Revolution grew from the distain of the Mexican people towards their President’s policies that favored the elite.

The October Revolution
(1917-1923)

New Planet by Konstantin Yuon (1921)

The October Revolutions resulted in the overthrowing of the Provisional Government in Russia and the establishment of Moscow as the new capital.

(1942)

Quit India Movement by Beohar Rammanohar Sinha (1952)

During World War II, the Quit India Movement rose up against the British who ruled India at the time. The movement was encouraged by Mahatma Gandhi.

(1948 until the early 1990s)

Untitled (Apartheid) by Keith Haring (1984)

The South African Apartheid was institutionalized racism that ensured the domination of the minority white population in South Africa.

Black Panther Party
(1966- Present)

We Shall Survive Without a Doubt by Emory Douglas (1971)

The Black Panther Party is a Black Power political organization that was created to defend and protect Black people in the United States.

Arab Spring
(2010-2012)

A painter uses his brush against a policeman armed with a mace photographed by Mona Abaza (n.d)

Graffiti and murals were very important during the Arab Spring protests, uprisings, and rebellions that occurred. Arab Spring resulted from an anti-government movement against oppressive regimes that were lowering the standard of living.

Click here to read more about the importance of graffiti during the Arab Spring.

Black Lives Matter Movement
(2013- Present)

Slam Poetry featuring various speakers

The Black Lives Matter Movement kicked off in 2012 after the death of a young man named Trayvon Martin. Martin was wrongfully murdered. Since then, attention has been raised in regards to police brutality and systemic racism the Black people currently and have always faced.

Yellow Vests Movement
(2018- Present)

Unknown Name by Pascal Boyart (PBOY) (2019)

The mural above was inspired by Liberty Leading the People. The Yellow Vests are made up of French citizens who have been protesting rising costs of living, include fuel prices. They claim that a large portion of the tax reforms in place were falling on middle and working class people.

Click here to learn more about the significance of the Yellow Vests Movement.

India's Farmer Revolt began towards the end of 2020. Farmers are revolting against new laws being put in place that they fear will hurt them. Hundreds of millions have gathered to fight against the unfair laws.
